Himalayan Red Rice

Unpolished grain, bran intact

Origin

Shimla hills

Elevation

1,400–2,400 m

Harvest

September to October

What makes it different

The bran layer is left on, which preserves the colour and the texture. Milling it off would make the grain cook faster, but it would also remove most of the reason to eat it.

Cool nights slow the grain down and set the anthocyanins that give it a deep russet tone.

Cooking guidance

  • Use a 1:2.5 rice-to-water ratio.
  • Cook 35 to 40 minutes, or soak first to cut the time roughly in half.
  • It works best in rajma-chawal, khichdi, or under a sauce-heavy dish.

Provenance

Shimla terrace farms

The crop is grown on rain-fed terraces with snowmelt irrigation and traditional smallholder methods, preserving the rhythm of the mountain seasons.
